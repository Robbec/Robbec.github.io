PhD in Engineering Science: Computer Science

On February 11, 2025, I obtained my PhD in Computer Science at KU Leuven, where I did research on Cross-Reality. With cross-reality, we want to bridge the gap between physical, augmented, and virtual realities by developing new interaction techniques and visualisations. I am currently also a part-time lecturer in extended reality at Thomas More. I have long been interested in how people interact with technology, and am looking for a full-time opportunity to put the skills I have acquired over the years into practice.

As a computer scientist I am familiar with Python and C++. During my PhD I was a teaching assistant for the course "datastructures and algorithms", which involved using Python. C++ is the programming language for Unreal Engine, which was used extensively during the PhD as well. Moreover, I have used Linux as my main operating system for about two years. During the PhD I have used video streaming solutions (WebRTC, and pixelstreaming which is specific to Unreal Engine), and I am currently preparing a workshop on 360 degree immersive video for Thomas More. I am familiar with AI and computer vision through classes in the master computer science, and have contributed to research on LLM-driven virtual agents (though not as the main researcher.) Futhermore, I followed courses on embedded systems, which I always found to be particularly interesting.

I have already been a research engineer at Flanders Make, prior to pursuing my PhD. I think the PhD has prepared me better for this kind of role, as it strengthened my ability to think creatively, and tought me how to effectively tackle complex problems.
