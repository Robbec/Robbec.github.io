PhD in Engineering Science: Computer Science

I recently received my PhD from KU Leuven, and am also a part-time lecturer extended reality at Thomas More, where I pass on my knowledge to a new generation of students.

My fascination with VR and AR originated with my master thesis in computer science, which was on redirected walking (a type of VR locomotion technique). After finishing my master in computer science I moved on to Flanders Make, where I worked in VR simulations of assembly cells and other 3D/Unity projects. Then I decided to pursue a PhD with prof. Adalberto Simeone, for which I conducted a more fundamental investigating into the concept of cross-reality.

During my PhD at KU Leuven I specialised in cross-reality, a type of mixed reality system that operates across multiple realities. This research was from a human-computer interaction perspective, and focused on how users interact with these cross-reality systems. Over the course of the past six years I have accumulated experiences in the design and development of a wide variety of XR systems, spanning multiple platforms (including most common engines like Unreal or Unity) and devices.

I am now looking for an opportunity to continue working with XR, but in a non-academic position with more real-world impact.
